About Ruby

One of the co-founders of Ruby, British Chinese entrepreneur Guo Bingzhong, graduated from the Economics Department of St. John's University in Shanghai. After China's reform and opening up, he returned to China to invest. As fresh cream cakes were still rare in China in the 1980s, which was already a common dessert in western countries, and China mainly produced artificial cream with high levels of trans fats, Guo saw a business opportunity.
In 1986, with the help of the Shanghai Jing'an District Overseas Chinese Affairs Office, Guo Bingzhong, the Shanghai Jing'an District Grain Bureau, and the Jing'an District Overseas Chinese Affairs Office established the Sino-British joint venture Red Ruby Food Co., Ltd. to produce fresh cream cakes and other pastries. Under the management and operation arrangement, Guo was responsible for importing the technology and equipment for making pastries from foreign countries, while the Chinese side was responsible for assigning personnel to manage the operation.
